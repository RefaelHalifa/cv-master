# CV Writing Standards & Information Gathering

## Information Gathering — Question Scripts

Ask these one at a time, in order. Never dump a full list at once.

### Section 1: Personal Info & Target Role
- "What's your full name as it should appear on your CV?"
- "What city and country are you based in?"
- "Which email address should appear on your CVs?"
- "And your phone number?"
- "Do you have a LinkedIn profile? If so, what's the URL?"
- "Do you have a GitHub profile you'd like included?"
- "What kind of role are you applying for — backend, frontend, full-stack, mobile, DevOps, something else?"
- "How would you describe your level — Junior, Mid, or Senior?"

### Section 2: Professional Summary
- "How would you describe yourself as a professional in 2–3 sentences?"
- If they struggle: offer to write it based on their info once you have the full picture.

### Section 3: Work Experience
- "Tell me about your work experience. For each role: company name, your title, the dates, and what you did."
- Push for achievements: "Did you improve anything, speed up a process, or work on something with measurable impact?"
- If no experience: "That's fine — tell me about freelance work, volunteer projects, or personal projects instead."

### Section 4: Technical Skills
- "What technologies do you work with? Walk me through your main programming languages, frameworks, databases, and tools."
- Follow up: "Which of these are you strongest in? Which have you used professionally vs personally?"

### Section 5: Projects
- "Tell me about any personal, academic, or freelance projects you're proud of."
- For each project: what it does, tech stack, your role, GitHub or demo link if available.

### Section 6: Education
- "What's your educational background? Include degrees, bootcamps, certifications — anything relevant."
- For each: institution, field of study, graduation year (or expected year).

### Section 7: Additional
- "What languages do you speak, and at what level?"
- "Anything else worth including — awards, publications, military service, volunteering?"

## Before Writing — Confirm Summary

Show a structured summary:
> "I have everything I need. Here's a quick summary of what I'll include — let me know if anything needs adjusting before I build the CV."

Wait for confirmation before generating.

## Tailoring Strategy

Each CV starts from the core profile but restructures emphasis for the target role.
Before writing any CV, identify the primary angle and strip or demote skills/projects
that dilute that angle.

### Common Angles
- **Backend** → emphasize server-side languages, APIs, databases, DevOps
- **Full-Stack JS** → emphasize React/Node.js, full lifecycle delivery
- **Mobile/iOS** → emphasize Swift, SwiftUI, shipped apps, UX awareness
- **GenAI/ML** → emphasize AI tooling, LLM APIs, data pipelines
- **Security** → emphasize offensive security, pentesting, threat modeling
- **Corporate/Big 4** → clean, conservative tone; omit culture-specific items

### Content Include/Exclude Logic
- Personal projects: include for startups/tech roles; consider excluding for conservative corporate
- Religious/cultural education: include for local market; exclude for international roles
- Training programs: can be listed as work experience when the program involved real dev work
- AI tooling: always include as genuine differentiator; frame as "daily workflow integration"

## Quantification Prompts

When a user gives a vague achievement, probe with:
- "How many users did it serve?"
- "Did it reduce time, cost, or errors — by how much?"
- "How large was the team or codebase?"
- "Was it deployed to production? For how long?"

## Languages Section

Always include all languages the user speaks. Multilingual candidates should always
list this — it is a genuine differentiator, especially for international companies.
